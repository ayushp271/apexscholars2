7 Things Indian Students Get Wrong About Applying to US Universities

Every year, strong Indian applicants get rejected from US universities they were qualified for — not because of their grades or test scores, but because of avoidable mistakes in how they approached the application. Here are the seven most common ones.

1. Starting too late.

US university deadlines — especially for Early Decision — can be as early as November 1st for a September intake. That means your SOP and essays need to be ready by October. Most students don't start until December. By then, early rounds are gone.

2. Applying to too few universities.

US admissions has become increasingly unpredictable. Even strong profiles face rejections at reach schools. Apply to a balanced list of 10–12 universities, not 4–5.

3. Writing a generic SOP.

The Statement of Purpose is where most Indian applicants lose ground. A generic essay that lists achievements without a compelling narrative gets ignored. Admissions officers read thousands of these. Yours needs to tell a story.

4. Ignoring financial aid until after admission.

Many US universities — especially liberal arts colleges — offer need-based aid that can dramatically reduce your cost. But you have to apply for it at the time of application, not after. Missing this can cost you lakhs.

5. Underestimating the importance of LORs.

Letters of Recommendation are not formalities. A generic "good student" letter hurts you. A specific, enthusiastic letter from someone who genuinely knows your work can tip the balance in your favour.

6. Not researching the specific program.

Saying you want to attend a university because it's "highly ranked" tells an admissions officer nothing. Your SOP needs to reference specific professors, research, courses, or opportunities that are unique to that institution.

7. Treating all universities as the same.

A Caltech application is completely different from a liberal arts college application — in what they value, what they ask for, and what makes a compelling case. One size fits none.
