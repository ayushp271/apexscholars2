THE PROCESS

We've Thought of Everything. So You Don't Have To.

A clear, step-by-step process from your very first question to the day you start your program.

1

Book Your Free Session

It starts with a conversation. You book a 30-minute session with one of our senior counsellors — online or in person. There's no fee, no commitment, and no sales pressure. Just an honest discussion about where you are and where you want to go.

2

Profile Evaluation

Your counsellor reviews your academic history, test scores, extracurriculars, work experience, career goals, and budget. We look at the full picture — because a great university match depends on far more than just grades.

3

Your Personalised Roadmap

Based on your evaluation, we build a custom plan. Which programs suit you. Which institutions to target. What your application timeline looks like. What tests you need, and when. What the realistic financial picture is. You leave this session knowing exactly what the next 12–18 months look like.

4

Shortlisting

We present you with a curated list of 8–12 universities or colleges — a balanced mix of aspirational, target, and safe options, backed by research and honest assessment of your chances at each one.

5

Application Support

This is where we do the heavy lifting together. SOPs, essays, LORs, application forms, portfolio guidance — every document goes through multiple rounds of review. Nothing is submitted without both of us being satisfied with it.

6

Offer & Scholarship Negotiation

When offers arrive, we help you evaluate them, compare financial aid packages, and — where possible — negotiate better scholarships. Getting into a great university AND funding it well is the goal.

7

Visa, Loan & Pre-Departure

Once you've accepted an offer, we guide you through visa documentation, connect you with education loan partners if needed, and run a pre-departure briefing so you're prepared for everything that comes next.

8

You Arrive. We Celebrate.

We stay in touch. Our alumni are part of the Apex Scholars community — and often our best source of advice for the next generation of students we work with.
