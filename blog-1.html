When Should You Start Planning to Study Abroad? (Most Students Start Too Late)

If you're in Class 12 and you've just started thinking about studying abroad, here's some honest news: you're behind the ideal timeline. Not irreparably — but behind. The students who get into the best universities with the best scholarships started planning in Class 11, sometimes earlier.

Here's why timing matters more than most students realise, and what to do depending on where you are right now.

12 months or more before your target intake

This is the sweet spot. At this stage, you can take your English proficiency test without pressure, retake it if needed, have multiple rounds of SOP drafts, research universities thoroughly, and apply in early decision rounds — which often have higher acceptance rates.

6–12 months before intake

Still very workable, but the pace picks up considerably. You'll need to move quickly on test preparation, start your SOP early, and be realistic about which institutions have deadlines you can still meet.

Less than 6 months before intake

It's not impossible, but your options narrow. Some universities accept rolling applications or have later deadlines. We've helped students in this situation — but it requires being focused and fast.

The bottom line: Start earlier than you think you need to. The planning process always takes longer than expected, and the students who aren't rushing produce better applications.
