IIM vs. Top MBA Abroad — Which Is Actually Right for You?

It's one of the most common questions we hear from ambitious postgraduate applicants: should I go for an IIM, or pursue an MBA abroad — at INSEAD, London Business School, or a top US school?

The honest answer is: it depends on what you actually want from an MBA. Here's a breakdown of the key differences.

Career Goals

If you want to work in India long-term — particularly in consulting, banking, or entrepreneurship — an IIM degree carries enormous weight domestically. If you want to build an international career, a top global MBA opens doors that even the best IIMs often don't.

Cost & ROI

IIM fees have risen sharply — flagship programs now cost ₹25–30 lakhs. A top international MBA can cost USD 100,000–200,000 all-in. The ROI equation depends on salary trajectory, location of work, and how quickly you repay your loan.

Timeline

IIM programs are typically 2 years. Many top international MBAs (like Oxford Saïd or INSEAD) can be completed in 12 months — a significant advantage for experienced professionals.

Profile Requirements

IIMs weight CAT score heavily. International schools look at GMAT/GRE, work experience, leadership, and diversity of background. If you have strong international work experience, an international application may actually be more competitive for you than a CAT application.

Our Take: Both paths are excellent. The question is which excellent path is excellent for you. A counselling session where we understand your goals, work experience, and financial situation will usually make the answer clear.
